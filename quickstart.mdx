## Welcome!

This guide will walk you through creating your first argument analysis from start to finish. By the end, you'll have generated a complete logical blueprint with fallacy detection and social sentiment analysis.

<Info>
  **Estimated time:** 5-10 minutes
</Info>

## Prerequisites

Before you begin, make sure you have:

- A modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge)
- An email address for account creation
- A topic, URL, or text you want to analyze

<Note>
  No technical knowledge required - this is a fully web-based platform.
</Note>

## Step 1: Create Your Account

First, you'll need to create a free account to access the platform.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Sign Up">
    Go to the Argument Cartographer homepage and click **Sign Up** in the top navigation.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter Your Details">
    Fill in the registration form:
    - **Email address** - Your working email
    - **Password** - At least 8 characters
    - **Display name** - How you'll be identified (optional)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Verify Email">
    Check your inbox for a verification email and click the confirmation link.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Complete Profile">
    Return to the platform and you'll be automatically logged in.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Use a strong, unique password. Your account will store all your analysis history.
</Tip>

## Step 2: Navigate to the Dashboard

After logging in, you'll land on the **Dashboard** - your analysis command center.

### Dashboard Layout

The dashboard consists of:

- **Analysis Input** - Large text area to submit topics, URLs, or text
- **Recent Analyses** - Your analysis history
- **Narrative Radar** - Trending pre-analyzed topics
- **Quick Stats** - Your usage metrics

<Card title="Dashboard Screenshot" icon="image">
  The dashboard features a clean, focused interface with the analysis input front and center.
</Card>

## Step 3: Submit Your First Analysis

Now for the exciting part - creating your first argument blueprint!

### Choose Your Input Type

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Topic Query">
    Enter a controversial topic or question:
    
    **Good examples:**
    - "Should artificial intelligence be regulated?"
    - "Universal basic income pros and cons"
    - "Climate change policy debates"
    - "Cryptocurrency as legal tender"
    
    **Tips:**
    - Be specific but not too narrow
    - Phrase as a question or statement
    - Avoid yes/no questions when possible
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Article URL">
    Paste a link to a news article, blog post, or opinion piece:
    
    **Supported sources:**
    - News articles (BBC, Reuters, CNN, etc.)
    - Blog posts and opinion pieces
    - Academic articles (if publicly accessible)
    - Government documents and reports
    
    **Example:**
    ```
    https://www.bbc.com/news/technology-67890123
    ```
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Raw Text">
    Paste the full text of an argument you want to analyze:
    
    **Best for:**
    - Debate transcripts
    - Academic essays
    - Opinion pieces
    - Social media threads
    
    **Paste directly into the input box** (up to 50,000 characters)
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Submit for Analysis

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enter Your Input">
    Type or paste your topic, URL, or text into the main input box.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click Analyze">
    Press the **"Generate Analysis"** button to start the AI processing.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Watch the Progress">
    You'll see real-time status updates:
    - 🔍 Searching web sources...
    - 📰 Scraping articles...
    - 🐦 Fetching social sentiment...
    - 🤖 Analyzing arguments...
    - ⚠️ Detecting fallacies...
    - ✅ Generating blueprint...
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Analysis typically takes **15-45 seconds** depending on topic complexity and source availability.
</Info>

## Step 4: Explore Your Analysis

Once processing completes, you'll see your comprehensive argument blueprint!

### Analysis Overview

The analysis view consists of three main sections:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Summary Panel" icon="file-text">
    High-level overview with:
    - Neutral summary
    - Credibility score (1-10)
    - Key takeaways
    - "Brutal honest take"
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Visualization Area" icon="diagram-project">
    Interactive argument map with:
    - 6 view modes
    - Clickable nodes
    - Export options
    - Full-screen mode
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Social Pulse" icon="rss">
    Twitter sentiment with:
    - AI-generated summary
    - Top 5 relevant tweets
    - Engagement metrics
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Understanding the Blueprint

<Steps>
  <Step title="Identify the Thesis">
    The **central contention** appears at the top in most views. This is the main claim being debated.
    
    Example: *"Artificial intelligence should be regulated by government agencies"*
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Explore Claims (For)">
    **Green/supporting nodes** represent arguments in favor of the thesis.
    
    Click any claim to see:
    - Full text
    - Source attribution
    - Supporting evidence
    - Detected fallacies
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review Counterclaims (Against)">
    **Red/opposing nodes** represent arguments against the thesis.
    
    These provide the counter-perspective and rebuttals.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Examine Evidence">
    **Evidence nodes** (smaller, attached to claims) contain:
    - Primary source quotes
    - Statistics and studies
    - Expert opinions
    - URL citations
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Switch Visualization Modes

Try different views to find what works best for your needs:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Flow View">
    Classical argument structure - thesis flows down through reasons and objections to conclusion.
    
    **Best for:** Understanding logical progression
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Balanced View">
    Split layout with "For" on left, "Against" on right.
    
    **Best for:** Comparing opposing arguments side-by-side
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Tree View">
    Hierarchical tree showing parent-child relationships.
    
    **Best for:** Tracing argument lineage
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Compass View">
    Circular layout with thesis at center, claims radiating out.
    
    **Best for:** Seeing the big picture at a glance
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Circular View">
    3D flip cards in circular arrangement.
    
    **Best for:** Interactive exploration and presentations
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Pillar View">
    Vertical columns for each side of the debate.
    
    **Best for:** Dense information display
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Tip>
  Use the **view mode selector** in the toolbar to switch between visualizations instantly.
</Tip>

## Step 5: Review Detected Fallacies

Scroll down to the **Fallacy Detection** section to see logical errors identified in the source material.

### Fallacy Card Breakdown

Each fallacy is presented with:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Severity & Category">
    - **Badge:** Critical (red), Major (orange), Minor (yellow)
    - **Type:** Logical, Rhetorical, or Statistical
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Problematic Text">
    The exact quote containing the fallacy, highlighted in context.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Explanation">
    Clear description of why this reasoning is flawed.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Educational Content">
    - Formal definition of the fallacy
    - How to avoid it in your own arguments
    - Example of the fallacy type
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Suggested Fix">
    A logically sound rephrasing of the argument.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Click any fallacy card to expand and see full educational details.
</Note>

## Step 6: Check Social Pulse

Open the **Social Pulse** panel (right sidebar) to see public discourse on the topic.

### What You'll See

1. **AI Summary** - Neutral synthesis of Twitter sentiment and key discussion points
2. **Top Tweets** - 5 most relevant/engaging tweets sorted by likes
3. **Author Info** - Profile pictures, names, and handles
4. **Engagement** - Visual indicators for likes, retweets, replies

<Tip>
  The Social Pulse helps you understand how the public is actually discussing the topic, beyond formal news sources.
</Tip>

## Step 7: Export & Share

Ready to share your findings? Use the export feature!

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Export Modal">
    Click the **Export** button in the toolbar (download icon).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose Format">
    Select your desired format:
    - **PNG** - High-quality image (1x, 2x, or 3x resolution)
    - **SVG** - Scalable vector graphic
    - **JSON** - Raw data for developers
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Configure Options">
    For PNG/SVG:
    - Color mode (Light or Dark)
    - Include background (Yes or No)
    - Resolution (1x, 2x, 3x)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Download or Share">
    - **Download** to save to your device
    - **Copy** to clipboard for quick pasting
    - **Share** directly to X, LinkedIn, WhatsApp, or Telegram
  </Step>
</Steps>

Use the **Ask More** chat feature to dive deeper into the analysis.

### How to Use Ask More

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Chat Panel">
    Click the **Ask More** button or chat icon in the toolbar.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Type Your Question">
    Ask anything about the analysis:
    - "What's the strongest evidence against the thesis?"
    - "Explain the straw man fallacy in claim 3"
    - "Which sources are most credible?"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Get AI Response">
    Gemini analyzes your question in context of the full blueprint and provides an informed answer.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Ask More has access to the entire blueprint, all sources, and detected fallacies - it can provide detailed, context-aware answers.
</Tip>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Analysis is Taking Too Long">
    If analysis exceeds 60 seconds:
    - Check your internet connection
    - Try a simpler topic
    - Refresh the page and try again
    
    If the issue persists, the API services may be experiencing high load.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="No Sources Found">
    If the system can't find sources:
    - Try rephrasing your topic with different keywords
    - Ensure your topic is recent and newsworthy
    - Check that it's a genuinely debated issue
    
    The system will fall back to AI knowledge with a disclaimer if external APIs fail.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Fallacies Seem Wrong">
    The AI is very sensitive and may occasionally:
    - Flag rhetorical flourishes as fallacies
    - Miss subtle logical errors
    - Misclassify fallacy types
    
    Use the confidence score (0-100%) as a guide. Lower confidence means the AI is less certain.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Export Not Working">
    If export fails:
    - Try a different format (PNG vs SVG)
    - Reduce resolution (3x → 1x)
    - Disable browser extensions that might interfere
    - Use Download instead of Copy to Clipboard
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
